01
Why a coach

You're not lazy. You're just doing it alone.

I've been there — the comebacks, the busy seasons, all of it. Here's what trying to figure it out solo tends to cost us, friend.

i.

Injuries that steal whole seasons

Random mileage and skipping your strength work is the fastest way to the sidelines. One injury can wipe out months of work — and the race you were so excited for.

ii.

A plan that isn't really yours

A free plan off the internet doesn't know your body, your history, or your crazy schedule. So you work SO hard on the wrong things and wonder why the PRs stopped.

iii.

Nobody cheering you on

When motivation dips or life gets full, there's nobody tweaking the plan, keeping you accountable, or reminding you you're ready. That's the lonely part — and where most of us quit.

You've already got the discipline to lace up. What you're missing is a plan that's truly yours — and someone genuinely in your corner.

Before you apply

Good questions.

The things runners ask me most, answered straight — no pressure, no jargon.

Do I have to be an experienced runner?
Not even a little. I coach everyone from first-time 5K runners and middle-school athletes to masters runners chasing lifetime PRs and Boston qualifiers. Your plan is built for exactly where you are — and a nervous first-timer gets the same care as a BQ. Promise.
I'm a sprinter / I run track — can you help?
Absolutely! Sprints are my world — my main event is the 400m and I'm still racing it. I coach the full range, 100m to the marathon. Your training is built specifically for YOUR event, not some one-size-fits-all distance plan.
How is the coaching delivered?
Online athletes get their plan through a coaching app, plus check-ins and text access based on your tier. In-person coaching is available locally in New Braunfels, TX, with daily-updated plans and in-person form work.
What if I get injured or my schedule blows up?
That's exactly why you have a coach. I adjust your plan as real life happens — travel, illness, busy weeks, niggles — so you keep moving forward safely instead of starting over.
Is it worth it if I could find a free plan online?
A free plan can't see your body, your history, or your crazy week — so you end up working SO hard on the wrong things. Coaching is the difference between just running and actually training toward something. And it costs less a day than your coffee.
Which plan should I choose?
If you're not sure, just apply below and I'll help you figure it out! I'll look at your goals and point you to the plan that fits your life and your budget — zero pressure.
